# BookmarX Light Editorial Theme

## Direction

BookmarX will use a clean editorial interface that keeps attention on saved
posts. The entire application, including the sidebar, uses light surfaces.
Color communicates hierarchy and intelligence without becoming decorative
noise.

## Palette

- `#0D132B` — primary text, headings, and high-contrast controls
- `#2563EB` — primary actions, links, active navigation, and focus states
- `#38BDF8` — sync progress and lightweight informational accents
- `#7C3AED` — AI, chat, summaries, and intelligence indicators
- `#E5E7EB` — application canvas, dividers, and subdued surfaces
- White — cards, menus, fields, and raised surfaces

## Visual System

- Use a soft gray canvas with white content surfaces.
- Keep borders crisp and shadows restrained.
- Use pale blue for active navigation and selected controls.
- Reserve purple for AI-specific meaning.
- Use cyan for live sync state and progress.
- Maintain generous spacing and an editorial typography hierarchy.
- Avoid dark panels, strong gradients, excessive pills, and ornamental color.

## Components

- Sidebar: white, subtle right border, pale-blue active rows, compact brand mark.
- Header: translucent white with a thin divider.
- Bookmark cards: white, navy text, subtle blue hover border and shadow.
- Filters: grouped controls on a quiet white surface with clearer labels.
- AI summaries: pale purple surface and purple intelligence label.
- Thread indicators: pale blue badge with a blue timeline marker.
- Sync progress: cyan and blue status treatment.
- Chat and briefs: document-like white surfaces with strong reading hierarchy.

## Accessibility and Responsive Behavior

- Preserve WCAG-friendly text contrast.
- Keep visible keyboard focus rings in bright blue.
- Do not rely on color alone for state.
- Preserve current responsive sidebar, grid, dialogs, and media behavior.

## Verification

- Run frontend lint and production build.
- Review the library, bookmark detail, chat, briefs, folders, and settings.
- Verify desktop and mobile layouts remain usable.
